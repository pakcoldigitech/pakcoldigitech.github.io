Introduction to the VN SS Commodore

One of Australia's most iconic performance vehicles from Holden.

1. Introduction to the VN SS Commodore

The VN SS Commodore, released in 1990 by Holden, is one of the most iconic and beloved models in Australian automotive history. As part of the third generation of the Holden Commodore, the VN series marked a significant shift in design and engineering for the brand. The SS (Super Sport) variant of the VN Commodore stood out as a performance-oriented model, combining luxury, style, and power. With a powerful 5.0-liter V8 engine, the VN SS delivered impressive performance on the road, earning a reputation for both its speed and handling capabilities. It quickly became a favorite among enthusiasts and collectors alike.

2. Performance and Engine Specs

The VN SS Commodore was powered by a 5.0-liter V8 engine, which produced around 165 kW (221 hp) of power and 400 Nm of torque. This engine allowed the SS variant to accelerate from 0 to 100 km/h in just under 7 seconds, which was impressive for its time. The V8 engine was paired with a five-speed manual transmission or a four-speed automatic, offering drivers a choice in how they wanted to experience the car. With a rear-wheel-drive layout and a well-tuned suspension system, the VN SS offered a balanced and enjoyable driving experience that made it a standout in the performance sedan market of the early 90s.
